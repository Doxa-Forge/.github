# **Doxa Forge**

**“Where Ideas Take Shape.”**

Doxa Forge is a **student-driven innovation lab** founded with a passion for **turning ideas into working prototypes and MVPs**. The mission is simple: **explore, experiment, and build real software solutions** that have the potential to grow into startups.

---

## **About Doxa Forge**

At this stage, Doxa Forge is currently a **one-person initiative** focused on **rapid prototyping and experimentation**. The goal is to:

* Transform ideas into **working prototypes or MVPs** quickly.
* Explore **AI, SaaS, web, mobile, and desktop applications**.
* Learn and apply **modern development practices, project workflows, and startup thinking**.

Once a prototype demonstrates potential, it may evolve into a **full-fledged startup or SaaS product**, either solo or with collaborators.

---

## **Current Focus Areas**

* **Rapid prototyping** – web apps, mobile apps, Electron desktop apps.
* **SaaS MVPs** – planning tools, team management, AI-driven workflows.
* **AI experimentation** – small-scale models, automations, or enhancements.
* **Idea validation** – turning concepts into testable software quickly.

---

## **Why Doxa Forge Exists**

* **To experiment:** Turn ideas into tangible software quickly.
* **To learn:** Improve skills in coding, design, AI, and project management.
* **To prepare for startups:** Develop prototypes that could scale into successful businesses.

**Doxa Forge is designed to grow**. Future collaborators, contributors, or co-founders will join as projects prove their viability.


---

## **Current Status**

* Active projects: experimental prototypes and MVPs
* Focus: turn ideas into usable software, validate feasibility
> All projects are **private** until ready to be shared, to protect ideas and early prototypes.

---

## **Future Vision**

* Grow Doxa Forge into a **student innovation hub** for building startup-ready prototypes.
* Bring in collaborators to **scale promising projects** into real businesses.
* Document processes, learnings, and best practices for **future members and contributors**.
